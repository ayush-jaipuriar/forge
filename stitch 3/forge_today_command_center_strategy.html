# Forge: Today & Command Center Design Strategy ## 1. Shared Visual Direction Forge is a unified OS. While the *purpose* of Today and Command Center differs, the *physics* of the interface remains constant. * **Shell Language:** A fixed left sidebar (Rail) anchors the application. A persistent header provides breadcrumbs and global system status (e.g., `FORGE // SYSTEM_READY`). * **Color Rules:** * **Base:** Near-black (`#0A0C10`). * **Surfaces:** Charcoal/Slate (`#1C2026`). * **Accents:** Forge Amber (`#FF8C00`) used strictly for "Action Required" or "Active State." * **Typography:** Inter Tight for headings (strong, editorial); JetBrains Mono for data points (precise, operational). * **State Semantics:** * **Amber:** Active/Primary Action. * **Muted Green:** Success/Optimal/Synced. * **Burnt Gold:** Warning/Stale. * **Oxblood:** Critical/Failed. --- ## 2. Today Page Design Strategy: The Execution Console The Today page is a **mission-critical workspace**. It must answer "What is my immediate priority?" within 2 seconds of viewing. * **Hierarchy:** 1. **Current Focus:** The single most important task or block right now. 2. **Operational Pressure:** Visualizing how the day's schedule is holding up. 3. **Readiness Pulse:** Quick status of body/mind (Sleep, Energy). * **Focal Zone:** The top-center of the screen is reserved for the "Active Mission." Everything else is supporting intelligence. * **Dominant vs. Quiet:** Active tasks are high-contrast with Amber accents; completed or future tasks are muted and lower in the surface hierarchy. --- ## 3. Today Page Layout Proposal (Desktop) * **Structure:** 3-Column "Operational Grid" (Fixed Max Width: 1440px). * **Left (25%):** **Readiness & Inputs.** Persistent quick-entry modules for physiological state (Sleep, Readiness score, Energy). * **Center (50%):** **The Execution Stack.** The "Active Block" at the top, followed by the chronological "Today's Mission" list. * **Right (25%):** **Intelligence & Pressure.** Real-time diagnostics: Time pressure, upcoming deadlines, and "War State" score. * **Spacing:** Tight 8px gutters between operational modules to create a "connected cockpit" feel. --- ## 4. Today Page Component Direction * **Active Focus Module:** A large, high-contrast surface. Title in bold 24px typography. Includes a "Live Timer" in Monospace. The only surface with a 2px Amber border. * **Recommendation Card:** A "Ghost Surface" (thin 1px border) that suggests the next best move based on current energy levels. * **War State Score:** A circular or semi-circular gauge that moves from Green to Gold as "Time Pressure" increases. * **Execution Action Buttons:** Rectangular, 4px radius. Primary is Solid Amber; Secondary is Slate Border. --- ## 5. Command Center Design Strategy: The Strategy Room The Command Center is about **patterns and trajectory**. It shouldn't feel like a "task list"; it should feel like a "diagnostic report." * **Identity:** High-density analytics that favor "Trend Lines" over "Status Checks." * **Clutter Prevention:** Grouping metrics by "Domain" (e.g., Performance, Physiology, Consistency). Use of "Progressive Disclosure"—click a chart to see the raw data. * **Tone:** Analytical, calm, and objective. Charts use Muted Green areas to define "The Performance Zone." --- ## 6. Command Center Layout Proposal (Desktop) * **Structure:** Triptych Layering. * **Top (Hero):** **Coach Insight Area.** A wide, editorial-style banner that summarizes the last 7 days in natural language (e.g., "Momentum is high, but recovery is lagging."). * **Middle (Primary):** **The Trajectory Grid.** 2x2 or 3x2 grid of high-density charts showing core KPIs (Execution Rate, Readiness vs. Effort). * **Bottom (Support):** **Momentum Heatmaps.** Calendar views and long-term consistency trackers. * **Chart Logic:** No "Spider Charts" or "Pie Charts." Use Line Charts for trends and Bar Charts for volume. --- ## 7. Command Center Component Direction * **Warning Cards:** Small, high-density modules that highlight a "bottleneck" (e.g., "Bedtime variance is +45m this week"). * **Projection Surfaces:** Dotted lines on charts showing "Predicted Path" based on current momentum. * **Momentum Modules:** High-density "GitHub-style" heatmaps for task completion streaks. * **Filter Controls:** A slim toolbar below the header for `7D`, `30D`, `90D` views. --- ## 8. Today vs. Command Center Contrast * **Today (Fast):** Large buttons, live timers, bold active states. Emotional goal: **Focus.** * **Command Center (Slow):** Smaller typography, dense charts, multi-metric comparisons. Emotional goal: **Clarity.** * **Overlap:** Both use the same sidebar, header style, and "System Status" indicators. --- ## 9. Desktop Monitor Optimization * **Anchoring:** Sidebars are fixed. Center content has a `max-width` of 1200px to prevent "The Long Row" effect. * **Composition:** Use of "Multi-Pane" layouts instead of a single long scroll. This mimics a professional dashboard (like Bloomberg or VS Code). * **Whitespace:** Not "empty space" but "structural breathing room" between major grid sections. --- ## 10. Mobile / Tablet Adaptation * **Today:** Stacks into a single column. "Readiness" moves to a top-horizontal scroll. "Execution Stack" becomes the primary vertical scroll. * **Command Center:** Charts become single-column cards. The "Coach Insight" remains pinned to the top. Filtering moves to a bottom sheet. --- ## 11. Design Tokens & Styles * **Typography Scale:** * Display: 32px (Command Center Headers) * Headline: 20px (Section Headers) * Body: 14px (Standard) * Data: 13px (Monospace) * **Radii:** 4px for buttons/chips; 8px for major panels. * **Borders:** 1px solid `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)`. --- ## 12. Implementation Priorities 1. **Phase 1 (Layout Shell):** Implement the 3-column Today grid and the Hero/Grid structure for Command Center. 2. **Phase 2 (Component Refactor):** Replace all pill-shaped elements with the new 4px/8px rectangular system. 3. **Phase 3 (Intelligence Layer):** Build out the "Coach Insight" banner and the "Momentum Heatmaps."
